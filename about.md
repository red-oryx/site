          <p class="eyebrow">About Red Oryx</p>
          <h1>A specialist consultancy for Ruby and Rails systems that need careful hands.</h1>
          <p>
            Red Oryx helps established businesses improve Ruby and Ruby on Rails systems that have become
            fragile, difficult to change, or risky to maintain.
          </p>
          <p>
            That often means legacy applications that still matter to the business but have become
            harder to work on over time. Features take too long to deliver, upgrades get postponed,
            tests are thin or unreliable, and internal workflows start to depend on code that nobody
            feels comfortable touching.
          </p>
          <p>
            Red Oryx exists to help bring those systems back under control. The focus is not on
            disruption for its own sake, and not on rewriting everything from scratch. It is on making
            important systems safer, clearer, easier to work on, and more sustainable over time.
          </p>
        </div>

        <p class="eyebrow">Services</p>
        <h2>What Red Oryx helps with.</h2>
        <p>Most engagements fall into one or more of the following areas.</p>

        <div class="about-services__grid">
          <article class="service-card">
            <span class="service-card__accent"></span>
            <span class="service-card__marker">01</span>
            <h3>Legacy Rails modernisation</h3>
            <p>Older Rails applications often become slow, fragile, and expensive to change. Red Oryx helps modernise them in a controlled way, reducing risk and complexity without defaulting to a full rewrite.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="service-card">
            <span class="service-card__accent"></span>
            <span class="service-card__marker">02</span>
            <h3>Refactoring and codebase stabilisation</h3>
            <p>When a codebase has accumulated years of pressure and patchwork, change becomes harder than it should be. Red Oryx helps untangle brittle areas, clarify responsibilities, and make the system safer to understand and extend.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="service-card">
            <span class="service-card__accent"></span>
            <span class="service-card__marker">03</span>
            <h3>Rails upgrades and technical recovery</h3>
            <p>Delayed upgrades have a habit of turning into business risk. Red Oryx helps teams work through Ruby, Rails, dependency, and platform upgrades in a structured way, leaving the system healthier afterwards.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="service-card">
            <span class="service-card__accent"></span>
            <span class="service-card__marker">04</span>
            <h3>Testing and confidence building</h3>
            <p>Many legacy systems lack the test coverage needed for safe change. Red Oryx helps introduce or strengthen meaningful automated tests so teams can refactor, upgrade, and release with more confidence.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="service-card service-card--featured">
            <span class="service-card__accent"></span>
            <span class="service-card__marker">05</span>
            <h3>Workflow and delivery cleanup</h3>
            <p>Sometimes the real problem is not just the code, but the way changes move around it. Red Oryx helps clean up release processes, delivery friction, and operational pain points so development becomes steadier and less painful.</p>
          </article>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section class="about-approach reveal">
        <p class="eyebrow">Approach</p>
        <h2>How Red Oryx approaches the work.</h2>
        <p class="about-approach__intro">The approach is pragmatic and business-aware, with the emphasis on safer progress rather than disruption for its own sake.</p>

        <div class="about-process">
          <article class="process-step reveal">
            <span class="process-step__number">01</span>
            <h3>Find the real friction</h3>
            <p>Start with the parts of the system creating the most delivery pain, technical risk, or operational drag.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="process-step reveal reveal--delay">
            <span class="process-step__number">02</span>
            <h3>Target useful change</h3>
            <p>Choose interventions that support the business, whether that means a focused audit, recovery work, or longer hands-on delivery.</p>
          </article>

          <article class="process-step reveal reveal--delay">
            <span class="process-step__number">03</span>
            <h3>Leave the system healthier</h3>
            <p>Improve structure, confidence, and maintainability without defaulting to rewrites or unnecessary technical theatre.</p>
          </article>
        </div>
      </section>
